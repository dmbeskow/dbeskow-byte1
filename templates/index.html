Using Network Centrality on Global Knowledge Graph Co-mentions to Determine Regional Influencers:

Nigeria Case Study

The primary question that this reseach seeks to answer is:

Who is the primary influencers at country and regional level?

In particular, who are the non-traditional and possibly little known influencers?

We attempt to answer this question by first aggregating the Global Knowledge Graph (GKG) which is part of the Global Database of Events Language and Tone (GDELT) family of data sets. This data contains hundereds of thousands of event and entity data from new reports. I subset this to a region or country of interest (in this case Nigeria) and then build a network based on people's names that are comentioned together in news articles. Once we build this network (in this case it contained over 16K nodes and almost 40K edges), we use several centrality measures (Degree, Betweenness, Closeness, and Eigenvector centrality) to determine those nodes that are at the 'center' of this graph.
